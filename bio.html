Biography

Early Life

I was born in Zurich, Switzerland, in May of 1993. Shortly after, my dad's work moved our family to Nashville, TN, where we lived for several years. When I was about 7 years old, we moved again to Greenville, SC, which is where we remained and where I did most of my growing up. When I was younger I experimented with many different sports including baseball, basketball, and soccer. I really stuck with soccer and played competitively up until I started high school. I also took lessons on the guitar and drums.

Drum Corps / WGI

From 2010 to 2013, I was a member of two world class competitive drumlines. Palmetto Percussion was an indoor percussion group that practiced and competed on the weekends during the winter. In 2010, we won overall in our class and were bumped up to become a world class group, where we competed for the next two seasons.

The Cadets is a drum corps that I was a member of in 2012 and 2013. Instead of just a drumline, a drum corps also has a brass section, colorguard, and front ensemble. 150 members and countless other staff/volunteers lived together for the entire summer, traveling across ~25 different states in buses and competing across the country. In 2013, we won the High Percussion award and the corps came in 3rd overall. This was an extremely rewarding experience. I learned a lot about hard work, discipline, and dedication.

College

After attending Greenville Technical College for a couple of years, I transferred to Clemson University to study Computer Science. I have learned so much here and I have really developed a love for computer programming and software development. Other than being a student, I have also worked for the university as 1) a teaching assistant in a computer science lab, and 2) an intern at Clemson Online, where I built and maintained different parts of the school's website.
